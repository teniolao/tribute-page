Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie

Nigerian writer

she was awarded the PEN Pinter Prize, named in honour of playwright and Nobel Laureate Harold Pinter.

Here's a time line of writer Adichie's life:

  • 1977 - Adichie was born in the city of Enugu in Nigeria
  • 1996 - She studied medicine and pharmacy at the University of Nigeria for a year and a half. During this period, she edited The Compass, a magazine run by the university's Catholic medical students
  • 2001 - As a black African in America, Adichie was suddenly confronted with what it meant to be a person of colour in the United States. She writes about this in her novel Americanah. She received a bachelor's degree from Eastern Connecticut State University
  • 2009 - Adichie spoke on "The Danger of a Single Story" for TED . It has become one of the top ten most-viewed TED Talks of all time with more than fifteen million views
  • 2009 - She got married to a Nigerian doctor who practiced in America and together they have a beautiful daughter
  • 2012 - she delivered the "Connecting Cultures" Commonwealth Lecture 2012 at the Guildhall, London. Adichie also spoke on being a feminist for TEDxEuston
  • 2019 - Multiple award-winning Nigerian writer Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie was named as one of the 100 Most Influential Africans by the Africa Report
